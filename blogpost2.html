How I Became A Data Scientist — No CS Degree, No Bootcamp

Published on January 7, 2024 by Egor Howell

It’s no lie that being a Data Scientist is arguably (in my humble opinion) one of the coolest jobs out there at the moment, especially with all the hype with AI this year.

In this article, I want to walk you through my whole journey to becoming a Data Scientist, shedding some light and advice on how you can become one yourself!

Quick note: My journey was to become a Data Scientist who specializes in Machine Learning. Data science means various things at different companies, so it’s important to understand what you want to be. However, for entry-level jobs, there is much muchness in this (a British saying).

They seem to have their own moods and personalities. Weather watchers around the world are often transfixed by their variability.

Coming from a physics background I pretty much had all the mathematical knowledge I needed:

  • Linear Algebra: Matrices, Eigenvalues, etc
  • Differentiation and Integration (Calculus)
  • Basic Statistics: Probability distributions, Linear Regression

The first course I took was Andrew Ng’s Machine Learning Specialization. I took it when it was still the 2012 version when the coding exercises were in Octave/Matlab.

Recently, it has been revamped and added more cutting-edge topics such as recommenders and reinforcement learning, and the exercises are all in Python

This course taught me the fundamentals of all the machine learning algorithms. This was all before I even started implementing them in code. I think building that intuition behind the algorithms is so invaluable.
